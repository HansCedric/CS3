The Importance of Education

The value of a proper education in the contemporary period couldn’t be understated. In the Philippines, an education will open up more opportunities in this developing nation and may very well be the path used to traverse the great mountains of poverty and gullibility that are unfortunately too often seen in the backgrounds of the regular Filipino’s life.

The Faults in The Philippine Education System

The present Philippine Educational System is weighted down by a noticeable lack of both quality and quantity of acceptable educational materials and teachers. It attempts to teach in two languages, causing a deficiency in communicational skills in some students in either the lingua franca of the world, English, or our beloved mother language, Filipino. Furthermore, this system faces all of these problems whilst being strangled at the neck by a low government budget.

The Long-Term Effects

A rotten educational system will be the greatest hindrance in our nation’s ascension. Without a system of education that cultivates a strong middle class, our country will be plagued by poverty, uncertainty, and ignorance. If we don’t act now, we will be undergoing troubling times that will intensify with every second of inaction that passes.
